Smart Compression Garment for Hypotension Prevention in Paraplegic Individuals

A closed-loop wearable system using force-resistive sensors to dynamically regulate compression pressure, preventing orthostatic hypotension and pressure sores in paraplegic patients.

Problem & Approach

Paraplegic individuals are prone to orthostatic hypotension due to impaired autonomic control. Compression garments help, but static pressure risks pressure sores. This project built a feedback-controlled garment that adjusts pressure in real time.

  • Integrated force-resistive sensors for continuous pressure monitoring.
  • Designed a closed-loop PID controller to regulate applied pressure within safe therapeutic bounds.
  • Validated pressure regulation in bench tests and controlled trials.
