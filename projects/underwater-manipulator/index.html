Low-Inertia Cable-Driven Underwater Manipulator

An industry-collaborative project with Oceaneering (Houston, USA) to design a cable-driven underwater manipulator with optimised distal loading, validated through gravity-compensated PID and optimal control in dynamic simulation.

Motivation

Underwater manipulators for inspection and intervention tasks face a fundamental tradeoff: more capable arms are heavier at the distal end, making them harder to control under hydrodynamic disturbances. Cable-driven mechanisms move actuators proximally, dramatically reducing distal inertia and enabling faster, more precise motion underwater.

Design & Approach

  • Designed the cable routing geometry to minimise distal loading through optimised pulley placement.
  • Developed gravity-compensated PID control for stable positioning under buoyancy and drag.
  • Implemented Optimal Control strategies using Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman-inspired methods to maximise tracking accuracy.
  • Validated all control architectures through dynamic simulation under realistic underwater disturbance models.
