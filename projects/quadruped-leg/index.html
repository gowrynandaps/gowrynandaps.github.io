Trajectory Planning & Control of a Low-Cost Quadruped Leg Design

A full-stack robotics project: designing a novel two-link quadruped leg with a Rotary Series Elastic Actuator (RSEA), building a simulation testbed in CoppeliaSim, and validating joint-space PD control on physical hardware driven by an ESP32 and ODrive motor controller.

Overview

Legged robots need actuators that can absorb impacts, measure ground forces, and generate compliant motion — especially at low cost. This project introduces a Rotary Series Elastic Actuator (RSEA) integrated into a two-link quadruped leg (L1 = 0.15 m, L2 = 0.225 m) to achieve greater impact tolerance and torque output than traditional rigid drives.

The work spans the full development pipeline: mechanical design in Fusion 360, dynamics modelling, CoppeliaSim simulation with torque-level actuation, and hardware bring-up with encoder-based feedback control.

System Architecture

  • Mechanical Design: Two-link leg (L1 = 0.15 m, L2 = 0.225 m) with RSEA at each joint; spring compression measured as torque proxy.
  • Sensing: 2400 CPR quadrature encoders for joint angle feedback; HX711 + YZC-516C load cell for ground reaction force measurement.
  • Electronics: ODrive motor controller in velocity mode, commanded over UART from ESP32 microcontroller.
  • Control: Cascaded joint-space PD controller with velocity saturation, gain-scheduled across swing/stance phases.
  • Simulation: Full CoppeliaSim testbed with Lua scripting and Python Remote API; multi-height gait trajectory validated in sim before hardware deployment.
