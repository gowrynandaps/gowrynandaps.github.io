Dynamic Characterization of an Underwater Flexible Single-Link Manipulator

Developed a finite element model of a flexible underwater manipulator as a rotating beam with deflection, and determined its resonance frequency using frequency-marching simulation.

Problem Statement

Flexible underwater manipulators exhibit complex dynamic behaviour — hydrodynamic drag, structural compliance, and coupling between rigid-body rotation and elastic deflection. Understanding resonance is critical to avoiding structural failure and control instability.

Modelling Approach

  • Modelled the manipulator link as a rotating Euler-Bernoulli beam with tip deflection, capturing the coupling between rigid-body motion and elastic deformation.
  • Discretised the beam using the Finite Element Method, assembling consistent mass and stiffness matrices including centrifugal stiffening effects.
  • Applied appropriate boundary conditions for the clamped-free rotating configuration.
  • Performed frequency-marching simulation to trace natural frequencies as a function of spin rate, identifying resonance crossings.
